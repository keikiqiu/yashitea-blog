I used to avoid Jiang Hua Xiang. Ginger Flower sounded spicy, and I am not usually a fan of spicy drinks. I was wrong.
Jiang Hua Xiang is one of the classic ten aromas of Dan Cong. The dry leaves smell like fresh ginger  warm, pungent, slightly sweet. But when brewed, the character changes completely. The ginger note softens and blends with floral undertones.
The taste is where it shines. A gentle warmth spreads across your tongue  not spicy like chili, but warm like ginger tea on a cold day. Underneath are notes of honey, white flowers, and lemongrass.
I drink Jiang Hua Xiang in the morning. It wakes up my senses in a way coffee never could. The warmth, the complexity, the evolving flavor across infusions  it is the perfect start to a day.
Brewing tip: Jiang Hua Xiang is more forgiving than other Dan Congs. It can handle higher temperatures and longer steeps without turning bitter. Great for beginners.
Frequently Asked Questions
Q: Is Jiang Hua Xiang actually spicy?A: Not like chili. It has a gentle warmth and gingery note, but it is smooth and floral.
Q: How many infusions?A: 8-12 infusions from good quality leaves.
