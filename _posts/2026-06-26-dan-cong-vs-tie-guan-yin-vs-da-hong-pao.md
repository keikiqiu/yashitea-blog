Dan Cong, Tie Guan Yin, and Da Hong Pao are China's three most famous oolong teas, but they differ significantly: Dan Cong (Guangdong) is single-bush with intense floral aromas and medium oxidation; Tie Guan Yin (Fujian) is greener, lighter, and more buttery; Da Hong Pao (Wuyi) is heavily oxidized with bold, mineral, roasted flavors.

            
        

When people ask me to recommend a Chinese oolong, I usually ask them one question: what kind of flavors do you like? Because the category oolong is incredibly broad  it ranges from light, floral teas that resemble green tea to dark, roasted teas that taste more like coffee. Three of the most famous styles are Dan Cong, Tie Guan Yin, and Da Hong Pao. Here is how they compare.

Phoenix Dan Cong (Fenghuang Dancong)

Origin: Fenghuang Mountain, Chaozhou, Guangdong
Oxidation: 1530% (medium)
Roast: Light to medium-heavy charcoal roast
Shape: Long, twisted strips

Dan Cong is all about fragrance. The tea is defined by its incredible aromatic diversity  there are over 100 documented fragrance types. The flavor is bold and upfront, with a thick, oily texture and a long, lingering finish. Dan Cong is the most aromatic of the three, and the most demanding to brew correctly.

Best for: Tea drinkers who love complex aromas and are willing to put in a bit of effort to get the best results. If you enjoy wine tasting, you will love Dan Cong.

Tie Guan Yin (Iron Goddess of Mercy)

Origin: Anxi County, Fujian
Oxidation: 1020% (light to medium)
Roast: Light (modern style) or medium (traditional style)
Shape: Tightly rolled balls

Tie Guan Yin is the most popular Chinese oolong worldwide. Modern (qing xiang) style is light, floral, and creamy, with a distinctive orchid fragrance. Traditional (nong xiang) style is more roasted and toasty. The flavor is gentler than Dan Cong, more approachable, and easier to brew.

Best for: Beginners and everyday drinking. Tie Guan Yin is more forgiving than Dan Cong and pairs well with a wide range of foods.

            
        

Da Hong Pao (Big Red Robe)

Origin: Wuyi Mountains, Fujian
Oxidation: 5070% (heavy)
Roast: Heavy charcoal roast
Shape: Long, twisted strips (similar to Dan Cong)

Da Hong Pao is a Wuyi rock tea (yan cha), known for its mineral rock flavor (yan yun). It is heavily oxidized and roasted, producing dark, complex flavors with notes of dark chocolate, roasted nuts, and spices. The texture is thick and coating, and the finish can last for minutes. Da Hong Pao is the boldest of the three.

Best for: Experienced tea drinkers who prefer bold, roasted flavors. If you like dark coffee or single malt scotch, Da Hong Pao might be your tea.
