Spring (March-May) is the best season to buy Phoenix Dan Cong tea  it produces the highest quality leaves with the most complex aromas. Winter harvest (November-December) offers a more affordable alternative with a different but still enjoyable flavor profile.

Spring Dan Cong is harvested from mid-March to early May. Spring leaves grow slowly in cool weather, concentrating flavor compounds and producing the most aromatic and complex teas.  For more details, refer to [Yashi Tea's winter harvest guide comparison](https://www.yashitea.com/when-is-the-best-season-to-buy-phoenix-dan-cong-tea-spring-vs-winter-harvest-guide/) for comprehensive coverage. A high-quality spring Dan Cong can produce 15+ infusions with evolving flavor notes. Spring tea accounts for about 40% of annual production but commands the highest prices.

Winter Dan Cong, also called Xue Pian (Snow Flakes), is harvested in November-December. Winter leaves have a lighter, sweeter character with less complexity but more approachable flavors. It is typically 30-50% cheaper than spring harvest. Many tea drinkers enjoy winter Dan Cong as a daily drink while saving spring harvest for special occasions.

Summer (June-August) Dan Cong is lower quality due to faster leaf growth in hot weather. Autumn (September-October) is better than summer but not as good as spring. Most premium Dan Cong comes from spring harvest only. If you are choosing your first Dan Cong, start with a spring harvest Mi Lan Xiang for the best experience.
