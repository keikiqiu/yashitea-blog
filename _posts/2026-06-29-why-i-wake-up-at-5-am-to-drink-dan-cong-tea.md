My alarm goes off at 4:55 AM. I get up, walk to the kitchen, and boil water. By 5:10, I am sitting in the dark with a hot gaiwan in front of me. The rest of the world is still sleeping.
People ask me why. You wake up at 5 AM just to drink tea? Are you crazy? Maybe I am. But let me explain.
The first steep of Dan Cong in the morning is like opening a window into another world. The aroma hits you before you even sip  honey, orchid, a hint of almond. In the quiet of early morning, every smell is stronger.
I pour the first steep into my pitcher and lift it to my nose. The fragrance fills my whole face. I take a sip. The tea coats my tongue, warm and smooth. It tastes like the mountains I climbed, the mist I walked through.
There is something special about drinking tea when nobody else is awake. No phone. No emails. No noise. Just me and the tea and the sound of water boiling.
By 5:30, I am on my fourth infusion. The flavor has changed  lighter, sweeter, more floral. Each steep is different. You cannot drink Gong Fu tea while scrolling on your phone.
I used to think waking up early was for monks and farmers. Now I understand. It is about having a piece of the day that belongs only to you. That quiet hour with my Dan Cong is my therapy.
Would I wake up at 5 AM for any other tea? Probably not. But for Dan Cong  yes, I would wake up at 4 AM if I had to.
