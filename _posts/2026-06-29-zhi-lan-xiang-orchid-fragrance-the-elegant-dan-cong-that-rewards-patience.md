I bought Zhi Lan Xiang because of the name. Orchid Fragrance. It sounded elegant. The kind of tea you drink when you want to feel like a Tang dynasty poet.
The reality exceeded the fantasy.
Zhi Lan Xiang is one of the more subtle Dan Cong varieties. It does not hit you over the head with fruit or honey. It whispers. The first steep is gentle, almost shy. But by the third infusion, the tea opens up. The orchid note becomes stronger, the texture thickens, the sweetness deepens.
This is a tea that rewards patience.
I have found Zhi Lan Xiang needs a longer rinse  about 20 seconds  to let the tightly twisted leaves begin opening. My first active steep is also longer, about 20 seconds instead of 10.
The result is worth the wait. Pale golden liquor with an aroma so refined it feels like drinking perfume (in a good way). Orchid dominant, with layers of white flowers, fresh grass, and a honeydew melon finish.
Not for casual drinkers. But for those who take the time, one of the most rewarding Dan Congs.
