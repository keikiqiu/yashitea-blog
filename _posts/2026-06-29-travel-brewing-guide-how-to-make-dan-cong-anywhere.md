Use a portable gaiwan or travel tea set. Pre-portion 5g of leaves into small bags. Use hot water from any source (hotel kettle, caf hot water tap). Even basic conditions can produce great Dan Cong with the right technique.

            
        
Why does this matter for Dan Cong quality?
Small adjustments make dramatic differences in the final cup. Dan Cong is sensitive to brewing parameters, and getting them right transforms the experience from mediocre to extraordinary.
What is the ideal approach?
Follow the standard Gong Fu parameters as a starting point, then adjust based on your specific tea and preferences. Every Dan Cong is slightly different.

            
        
How do I know when I have it right?
The tea should taste bright, aromatic, and smooth without any harshness or bitterness. The aftertaste should be sweet and lingering. If you get this consistently, you have found your parameters.
