I remember staring at online tea shops, completely overwhelmed by the choices. Gaiwan sizes. Teapot materials. Cup shapes. Pitcher styles. I had no idea what to buy.

            
        
After years of trial and error (and wasted money), here is what I wish someone had told me.
Start with a gaiwan, not a teapot. Gaiwans are cheaper, easier to clean, and give you more control over your brewing. A 120-150ml porcelain gaiwan costs $10-20 and will serve you for years. Do not buy a Yixing clay pot until you know what teas you love  different clays suit different teas, and the wrong pot can ruin the flavor.
Get a fairness pitcher. A gong dao bei (fairness pitcher) ensures every cup tastes the same. Without one, the first cup is weaker and the last cup is bitter. A simple glass pitcher costs $5-10.

            
        
Cups matter more than you think. Small cups (30-50ml) keep the tea hot and concentrate the aroma. Tall, narrow cups are best for smelling. Wide, shallow cups cool the tea faster. I use porcelain cups for daily drinking and thin celadon cups for special teas.
Accessories you actually need: A tea tray (cha pan) to catch spills. A tea towel. A strainer if your gaiwan has wide gaps. That is it. You do not need a tea pet, a tea table, or a bamboo mat. Add those later if you want.
Total cost for a beginner setup: $30-50 for everything you actually need. Ignore the $200 starter kits.
Frequently Asked Questions
Q: Should I buy a full tea set or piece by piece?A: Piece by piece. Full sets often include items you do not need. Buy a gaiwan + pitcher + 2 cups + strainer.
Q: Porcelain or clay gaiwan?A: Porcelain for beginners. It does not absorb flavor, so you can brew any tea in it. Clay is for advanced drinkers.
