The main difference between Phoenix Dan Cong (Guangdong) and Wuyi oolong (Fujian) is that Dan Cong is lightly roasted with intense floral aromas from single-bush cultivars, while Wuyi oolongs like Da Hong Pao are heavily roasted with bold mineral and stone fruit flavors from a rocky terroir.

            
        

Origin & Terroir

Dan Cong comes from Fenghuang Mountain in Guangdong at 800-1500m elevation with volcanic soil. Wuyi oolongs come from the Wuyi Mountains in Fujian, where tea grows in rocky crevices with mineral-rich soil  this unique rock tea (Yan Cha) character is what sets Wuyi oolongs apart.

Processing Differences

Dan Cong is medium-oxidized (30-50%) with lighter roasting to preserve natural floral aromas. Wuyi oolongs are also medium-oxidized but undergo heavier charcoal roasting, which creates the signature mineral and caramelized flavors. Both use complex traditional processing methods that require master tea makers.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Dan Cong if you want intense floral aromas and a lighter, more elegant tea experience. Choose Wuyi oolong if you prefer bold, roasted, mineral flavors. Many tea drinkers enjoy both  and if you are new to Chinese oolongs, start with Mi Lan Xiang Dan Cong (sweeter) or a lighter Wuyi oolong to see which style you prefer.
