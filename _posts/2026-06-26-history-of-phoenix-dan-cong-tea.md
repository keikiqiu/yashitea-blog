Phoenix Dan Cong tea has over 600 years of history, dating back to the Song Dynasty (960-1279 AD). Legend says the Song emperor fled south to Fenghuang Mountain, where local tea farmers presented him with this unique oolong. The name "Dan Cong" means "single bush"  each tea bush is cultivated individually for its unique flavor.

            
        

I have always believed that the best teas have the best stories. Phoenix Dan Cong is no exception  its history stretches back over six centuries, intertwined with imperial dynasties, migrating tea masters, and a mountain that seems to have been designed specifically for growing extraordinary tea.

The Beginning: Song Dynasty (9601279 AD)

The story begins in the Song Dynasty, when tea cultivation first spread to the remote Fenghuang Mountain region of Chaozhou. Local farmers discovered that the misty, high-altitude environment was perfect for tea. The original tea plants were not cultivated  they were wild trees growing among the mountain forests, some of which still exist today.

According to local legend, a Song emperor fleeing invading forces took refuge in Chaozhou and was served tea from these mountain trees. He was so impressed that he declared it a tribute tea  reserved for the imperial court. Whether the legend is true or not, Dan Cong production has been continuous since at least the 13th century.

The Ming Dynasty Refinement (13681644)

During the Ming Dynasty, tea culture in China underwent a major transformation. Loose-leaf tea replaced compressed tea cakes, and the art of oolong processing began to develop. Chaozhou tea masters started experimenting with oxidation levels and roasting techniques, laying the foundation for what would become Dan Cong.

This period also saw the emergence of the single bush (Dan Cong) philosophy. Farmers noticed that different tea bushes produced leaves with distinctly different aromas. Instead of blending them together, they began processing each bushs harvest separately to preserve its unique character. This practice continues to this day.

The Qing Dynasty Golden Age (16441912)

The Qing Dynasty was the golden age of Dan Cong. Tea production expanded dramatically across Fenghuang Mountain. Farmers planted thousands of new tea bushes, and the reputation of Chaozhou tea spread across China and into Southeast Asia through the thriving tea trade.

            
        

It was during this period that the famous Ten Fragrance Types were first categorized. Tea merchants needed a way to describe the different aromas, and the poetic naming tradition  Honey Orchid, Gardenia, Osmanthus, Almond  was born. The most famous names from this era are still used today.
