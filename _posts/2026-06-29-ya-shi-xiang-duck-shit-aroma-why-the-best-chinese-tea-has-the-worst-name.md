Let me be honest. The first time I heard the name Duck Shit Aroma, I laughed. Then I wrinkled my nose. Then I bought some anyway.
Best decision I ever made.
Ya Shi Xiang is the most famous Phoenix Dan Cong variety. Local legend says a farmer named it this way to keep it secret  nobody would steal a tea called duck shit. Another version says the soil where it grows was fertilized with duck droppings, making the leaves uniquely mineral-rich.
The tea itself? Nothing like the name suggests. When I first opened the bag, the dry leaves smelled like honey and wildflowers. I brewed it  5 grams, 95C, 10 seconds. The liquor came out bright golden. Orchids. Honey. Apricot.
I took a sip. The mouthfeel was thick and creamy. The flavor hit me in waves: first floral, then fruity, then a long sweet finish that lingered for almost a minute.
This is not a joke tea. This is one of the most sophisticated oolongs I have ever tasted. Real Ya Shi Xiang from Phoenix Mountain is dark and twisted, intensely floral with orchid being the dominant note, sweet and layered. Good Ya Shi Xiang can be brewed 10-12 times.
I once served it to a friend without telling him the name. He loved it. When I told him, his face went through five expressions in three seconds. I hate that I still want to drink it, he said.
If you are new to Dan Cong, Ya Shi Xiang is the perfect starting point. Just do not judge it by its name.
