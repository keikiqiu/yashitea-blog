Gongfu Cha (工夫茶), which translates to "making tea with skill," is a traditional Chinese tea ceremony that elevates tea brewing into an art form. Unlike Western-style tea brewing, Gongfu Cha uses a higher leaf-to-water ratio and multiple short steeps to extract the full flavor profile of premium teas.

## The Essential Teaware

To practice Gongfu Cha, you'll need:

- **Gaiwan or Yixing Teapot**: A gaiwan (covered bowl) is perfect for beginners, while a Yixing clay pot is preferred for aged pu-erh and oolongs
- **Cha Hai (Fairness Cup)**: Ensures consistent strength across all servings
- **Tea Cups**: Small, handleless cups that allow you to appreciate the tea's aroma and color
- **Tea Tray**: Catches water and spills during the brewing process
- **Tea Pitcher**: For warming and rinsing vessels

## Step-by-Step Gongfu Brewing

### 1. Warm the Vessels
Pour hot water over your teaware to warm it up and remove any residual odors.

### 2. Rinse the Tea
Add tea leaves to the gaiwan (about 5-7g for a 150ml gaiwan), pour hot water, and immediately discard — this "awakens" the leaves.

### 3. First Steep
Pour water at the appropriate temperature and steep for 10-20 seconds. The first steep is often the most aromatic.

### 4. Subsequent Steeps
Add 5-10 seconds for each subsequent steep. Premium oolongs can be steeped 7-10 times!

### 5. Serve and Enjoy
Pour into the fairness cup, then into individual cups. Appreciate the aroma before tasting.

## Tea Temperature Guide

| Tea Type | Temperature | Steep Time |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Green Tea | 75-80°C | 15-20s |
| White Tea | 85-90°C | 20-30s |
| Oolong | 95-100°C | 10-20s |
| Pu-erh | 100°C | 10-15s |
| Black Tea | 90-95°C | 15-25s |

## Why Gongfu Cha?

Gongfu brewing allows you to experience the complete evolution of a tea's flavor — from the first bright, floral notes to the deeper, sweeter infusions later on. It's a meditative practice that connects you with centuries of Chinese tea culture.
