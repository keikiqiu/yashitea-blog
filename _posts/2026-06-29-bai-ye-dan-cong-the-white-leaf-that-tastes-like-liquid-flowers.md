I first heard about Bai Ye from a tea vendor in Chaozhou. White Leaf, he called it. The most floral Dan Cong we produce. I was skeptical. Every Dan Cong claims to be floral.
Turns out he was right.
Bai Ye (white leaf) is named for the pale green color of fresh leaves before processing. The cultivar produces leaves with higher concentrations of aromatic compounds. When I first brewed it, the aroma was overwhelming  like walking into a greenhouse full of jasmine and gardenias. But not heavy or perfumed. Light, airy, ethereal.
The most remarkable thing about Bai Ye is the texture. The liquor is thin and silky, almost weightless. But the flavor intensity is high. A fascinating contrast.
Bai Ye is also one of the most forgiving Dan Congs. It rarely turns bitter. Perfect for Gong Fu beginners still learning their timing.
I keep Bai Ye for impressing guests. It is not the most complex Dan Cong, but it is the most beautiful. One sip and people understand why tea drinkers chase the perfect cup.
